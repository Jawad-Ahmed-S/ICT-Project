    Benefits of HIIT

    • Burns more calories in less time
    • Increases cardiovascular health
    • Improves endurance and stamina
    • Promotes fat loss and muscle preservation
    • Boosts metabolism even after the workout is done

    Types of HIIT Exercises

    • Bodyweight Exercises: Jump squats, burpees, mountain climbers, high knees
    • Cardio HIIT: Sprints, cycling, rowing, or jumping rope
    • Strength Training HIIT: Push-ups, lunges, dumbbell presses, kettlebell swings
    • Tabata Training: A specific form of HIIT with 20 seconds of work followed by 10 seconds of rest

    Tips for Getting Started with HIIT

    • Start slow and gradually increase intensity to prevent injury
    • Begin with 2-3 HIIT sessions per week
    • Focus on form and technique during high-intensity intervals
    • Include a warm-up and cool-down to protect your muscles
    • Listen to your body and take rest days as needed
